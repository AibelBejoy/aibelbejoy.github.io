History: The school was established in Elthuruth in 1889 under the St. Mary's Ashram founded by Chavarayachan. What was initially a pre-primary school was gradually upgraded to a higher secondary school. St. Aloysius is unique in having KG to PG under one roof. The founding father of CMI Sanyasbha, V. A primary school and boarding house named after St. Aloysius was started in 1889 in connection with the monastery of Elthuruth, which was laid the foundation stone by Chavara Kuriakos Eliasachan on February 2, 1858. About 300 students from different parts of Kerala stayed and studied in St. Aloysius, which was the first residential school in Thrissur. In 1913 the Maharaja of Kochi laid the foundation stone for a three-storey building for the Rama Varmathampuran High School and St. Aloysius grew into a High School in 1933 and a Higher Secondary School in 2001. During the caste system, it was also a center of education that provided education to the upper classes without any serious discrimination of religion. The High School celebrated its Golden Jubilee in 1983 and celebrated its Platinum Jubilee in 2008 by completing 75 years. The school is preparing to celebrate Navati in 2023

Physical facilities: The school is situated on three acres of land. High School has 13 classrooms and UP has 9 classrooms in one building. The school has a very spacious playground. High School and Higher Secondary have separate computer labs. The high school has about 15 computers. Broadband internet facility is available in both the labs.
